REAL-WORLD APPLICATIONS

How This Powers Real Trading Decisions

⚙️

Options Pricing

Forecasted σ̂ inputs directly into Black-Scholes / Heston model to price near-dated BTC/ETH options more accurately than static IV surfaces.

Helps traders fairly price contracts that let them bet on future price moves without actually owning the asset.

C = S·N(d₁) − K·e−rT·N(d₂)
σ̂ → Fairer option prices → Better bets
🛡️

Delta Hedging

Rising σ̂ triggers aggressive Δ rebalancing before drawdowns materialise, reducing P&L bleed.

When risk rises, smart traders adjust their positions to protect profits — like tightening your seatbelt before a bumpy road.

ΔRebalance ∝ σ̂t+1
📉

VaR & Margin

99% 1-day VaR = 2.33 × σ̂_daily × Position. Dynamic and forward-looking vs static historical vol.

Estimates the most you could lose in one day with 99% certainty — so you always know your worst case before it happens.

VaR₉₉ = 2.33 · σ̂ · P
🔀

Regime Filters

If VIX leads importance → macro hedges (long VIX calls, short SPY). If MACD/RSI lead → crypto momentum overlays.

The AI identifies whether fear or momentum is driving the market and adjusts its strategy recommendations to match.

VIX → macro hedge | RSI → momentum overlay

DOCUMENTATION

📄 Project Documentation

Full technical and conceptual walkthrough of the ApexChain pipeline.

1. Problem Statement

Crypto markets exhibit extreme non-linear volatility clustering that classical time-series models (ARIMA, GARCH) struggle to capture due to structural breaks and high-dimensional macro interactions. ApexChain addresses this by framing volatility prediction as a supervised regression task on rich feature sets, combined with unsupervised regime detection, to produce actionable BUY/SELL/HOLD signals anchored in derivatives finance.

2. Data Sources & Feature Engineering (27 Features)

Data: yfinance — user-specified ticker + ETH-USD + ^VIX + SPY. 80/20 chronological split.

  • Log Returns: LogRet, ETH_LogRet, SPY_LogRet, LogRet_lag1/2/3
  • Target — RV7: 7-day rolling σ of log returns × √252 (annualised realised volatility)
  • RV Lags: RV7_lag1, lag2, lag3, lag5, lag7 (autoregressive features)
  • RSI-14: Relative Strength Index, 14-day period
  • Bollinger Bands: BB_Width (4σ/SMA), BB_PctB (%B position)
  • MACD: MACD_Line (EMA12−EMA26), MACD_Signal (EMA9), MACD_Hist
  • Moving Averages: SMA_20, EMA_12, Price_vs_SMA20
  • VIX: VIX level, VIX_lag1, VIX_lag3
  • Macro: SPY_Mom5 (5-day SPY momentum), ETH_Corr5 (5-day ETH-ticker rolling correlation)
  • Behavioral: FearGreed — VIX-inverse proxy with noise (API-ready placeholder)
  • Volume: Raw trading volume

3. Model Architecture

Supervised (Regression target = RV7):

  • Random Forest Regressor — GridSearchCV over n_estimators=[200,400], max_depth=[5,8], min_samples_leaf=[3,5]
  • XGBoost Regressor — GridSearchCV over n_estimators=[200,300], max_depth=[3,5], learning_rate=[0.05,0.1], subsample=[0.8,1.0]
  • CV: TimeSeriesSplit(n_splits=5) — strictly no future data in folds
  • Preprocessing: StandardScaler fit only on train set

Unsupervised:

  • K-Means(n_clusters=3) — regime detection; clusters mapped by avg RV → Bull / Sideways / Bear
  • PCA(n_components=2) — 27-dim → 2D for regime scatter visualization

4. Evaluation Methodology

Both models evaluated on a strictly held-out 20% chronological test set. Metrics: MAE and RMSE on annualised RV (× 100 for % display). Champion = lower MAE on test set. TimeSeriesSplit used in GridSearchCV to replicate walk-forward validation and prevent temporal leakage.

5. Signal Scoring System

5-factor linear scoring model: σ̂ trend (±2), RSI (±2), MACD Histogram (±1), VIX level (±1), K-Means regime (±1). Max absolute score = 7. Signal: score ≥ 3 → BUY, ≤ −2 → SELL, else → HOLD. Confidence = min(100, 50 + |score| × 10)%.

6. Finance Connection & Real-World Applications

The 7-day RV forecast (σ̂) feeds directly into: (1) Black-Scholes / Heston options pricing as the σ input; (2) Dynamic delta-hedging — a rising σ̂ triggers more aggressive Δ rebalancing; (3) VaR computation — 99% 1-day VaR = 2.33 × σ̂/√252 × Position; (4) Regime-based overlay strategies — VIX-led regimes favor macro hedges, RSI/MACD-led regimes favor momentum overlays.

7. Limitations & Future Work

  • Fear & Greed Score is currently a VIX-inverse proxy; production version should call the Alternative.me API
  • Model retraining is per-request; production would use scheduled retraining with model registry
  • Signal scoring weights are heuristic; future work: calibrate via backtesting on historical signals
  • No on-chain data (exchange flows, whale wallets) — strong alpha source for crypto specifically
  • LSTM or Temporal Fusion Transformer could capture longer temporal dependencies

8. Risk Assessment Methodology

The risk tolerance slider modifies signal generation thresholds in two ways: (1) it adjusts the RSI buy/sell trigger points — conservative investors require a deeper oversold condition (RSI < 25–30) before a BUY fires, while aggressive investors act on shallower dips (RSI < 45–55); (2) it raises or lowers the composite signal score threshold required to generate a BUY recommendation. This reflects the real-world practice of risk-adjusted position sizing and entry criteria, where investor risk appetite directly governs trade execution rules. RSI serves its dual role here as both an ML feature (input to XGBoost/Random Forest) and a direct risk signal (threshold-based rule in the scoring engine).
